The problems on this site were numerous: poor layout, drab color choices, enourmous misuse of white-space, and in general just bad design. The whole site was set up in a 3 coulmn layout, with 2 persistant sidebars, the left of which changed based on the page to hold either a tiny bit of text, of images that belonged in the main section. The right sidebar never changed and holds reviews for the author's books.

The only option that I could see for this site was a complete overhaul from the ground up.

I started with a background image for the header based off of cover art for the first 2 books in the Hunger Games series. I blended them together, then removed the dark areas and set a gradient background across the whole body that transitions subtley from one color tone to another, tying the 2 artworks together further.

I then added a fixed, scaling Heading and Subheading, Navigation at the top, and a fixed position, scaling, pixel-density responsive logo that helps to bridge between the header and the main content of the page.

For the pages, I tried to keep them mostly similar categories to what the original site had, though I renamed the "Interview" tab to be Blog, with the intent of making it a placeholder for an actual blog to be developed later. I nested the actual Interview page as a faux blog post in a submenu.

The home page I turning into a "Coming Soon" landing page, though I did add the responsive slider at the bottom, as per the directions. To be honest, I probably would not have the slider on this page in a real project, though it could easily become the basis for a gallery page.

For the Books page, I made sections for the different types of books she has written, and made info cards with the covers, the reviews she had in the sidebar, and a hover animation the overlays the cover with a short blurb about each particular book.

The About The Author page is a basic bio with a wrapped picture of the author that is responsive.

The faux blog post is where I decided to use the lazy-loading gallery, though I made it into a lazy loading background image gallery behind the post. The taller the post, the more images are allowed to show, and they only load when you scroll to them. I couldn't think of a better place to put in this requirement without making a whole new media gallery page.

Finally we have a basic Contact page, with a responsive, if basic, form that takes you to a thank you page on submission.

The most challenging part of this particular assignment was making the header, the header background, and the logo all move and scale together to keep consitency across multiple device widths. I learned a lot about scaling text that I didn't know before, as well as nesting positioning and starting with assests that are properly sized.

Working with this different color pallete also presented some challenges, but I think that it turned out well and I enjoyed it.
